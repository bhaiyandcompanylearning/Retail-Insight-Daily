Lakme Plans Aggressive Mid-Year Discount Push After Market Share Slips in Color Cosmetics Segment

RB
Retail & FMCG Bureau May 28, 2025 · Mumbai
6 min read

Lakme is preparing to roll out an aggressive mid-year retail campaign across India after internal sales tracking indicated a noticeable slowdown in its color cosmetics segment during Q1 2025, according to industry sources familiar with the company's distribution strategy.

The company is expected to introduce a high-visibility nationwide discount program in June, aimed at strengthening its presence in blush, face glow, and compact makeup categories—segments that have recently witnessed intensified competition from fast-growing beauty brands.

Industry analysts say the move comes as several emerging beauty brands have gained consumer traction through influencer-led marketing and quick-commerce distribution.

"Color cosmetics has become one of the most competitive battlegrounds in 2025. Brands that win social trends win retail shelves," said a beauty market analyst based in Bengaluru.

Retail chain partners have reportedly been urging legacy brands to offer deeper promotions to drive walk-ins during the summer season, which traditionally sees a dip in discretionary makeup spending.

"Summer is not naturally strong for makeup compared to festive quarters. Retailers need campaigns to generate demand," said a category manager at a leading national retail chain.

Distributors also suggest Lakme's planned campaign may be part of a broader inventory rebalancing effort, clearing older packaging stock and creating space for upcoming festive product lines.

People familiar with the rollout say the campaign will likely feature a "glow"-themed branding strategy and could include significant discounts, bundling offers, and expanded in-store displays across key North and South India retail hubs.
