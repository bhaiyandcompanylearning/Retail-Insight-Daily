Lakme Signs Expanded Modern Trade Partnership to Boost Tier-2 Reach, Plans National Visibility Drive

FS
FMCG Strategy Desk May 22, 2025 · Mumbai
4 min read

Lakme is expanding its modern trade footprint as part of a new retail partnership strategy aimed at boosting distribution across Tier-2 markets, according to trade sources.

The initiative is expected to strengthen Lakme's in-store presence across multiple national retail chains, with dedicated focus on cosmetics categories that have seen rapid demand growth, including blush and face glow products.

Industry insiders say the brand has been renegotiating shelf placement agreements and exploring store-level promotional programs tied to performance targets.

"Brands are now competing for physical visibility again, but the new metric is speed of sales. Lakme is aligning modern trade strategy with sell-through campaigns," said a distributor partner in North India.

The company is also expected to increase in-store branding materials, tester counters, and bundled promotional packs as part of the rollout.

"Modern trade expansion is rarely done without promotional campaigns. If Lakme is scaling shelves, they will run a high-impact campaign to generate velocity," a senior retail supply chain manager said.

Experts believe this move signals Lakme's intention to regain category dominance, especially in stores where newer digital-first competitors have recently gained traction.
