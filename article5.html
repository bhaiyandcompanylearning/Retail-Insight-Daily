Cosmetics Supply Chain Faces Dispatch Pressure as Warehouse Tech Upgrades Disrupt Operations in South Zone

SL
Supply Chain & Logistics Desk June 3, 2025 · Hyderabad
5 min read

Warehouse modernization projects across India's FMCG sector are creating short-term operational disruptions, particularly in high-volume distribution hubs in the South, according to logistics and supply chain executives.

Multiple warehouses serving consumer goods and beauty products are undergoing system upgrades involving new inventory tracking modules and automated dispatch planning systems.

While these upgrades are expected to improve long-term accuracy and efficiency, short-term execution challenges are already being reported.

"A warehouse system migration often results in delayed dispatch windows and partial stock visibility issues," said a logistics consultant who works with multiple FMCG clients.

Several distributors in South India reported that dispatch turnaround times have become inconsistent since late May, with some shipments being held due to scanning failures, order sync mismatches, or temporary downtime.

Industry experts warn that if such disruptions coincide with a demand spike caused by a nationwide promotional campaign, retail stores could face short-term replenishment gaps.

"Demand surges expose every weakness in the supply chain. If warehouses are in transition mode, service levels can drop fast," the consultant added.

While no specific company was named, sources suggest that multiple beauty and personal care brands operating in the region are likely to face stress in June-July as promotional volumes rise.
