Indian Retailers Demand Higher Promotional Support as Summer Footfall Drops Across Beauty Category

RM
Retail Market Watch May 10, 2025 · New Delhi
5 min read

Retailers across India are pushing FMCG and cosmetics brands to offer stronger promotional support after a softer-than-expected summer season impacted category footfall in beauty and personal care.

According to retail executives, the beauty category—particularly color cosmetics—has seen slower growth in March-April 2025 compared to last year, largely due to inflation-driven discretionary spending cuts and shifting consumer priorities.

"Skincare is still moving, but makeup slows down unless it's tied to an event or a strong promotion," said a merchandising lead at a large-format retail chain.

Multiple modern trade partners are now demanding higher trade margins, deeper consumer discounts, and in-store visibility support, especially for face makeup categories such as blush, compact powder, and highlighter products.

Retailers noted that brands that fail to offer promotional programs are losing shelf priority to competitors willing to invest in display counters and price-off schemes.

"Without discounting, you don't see high velocity. Brands must create reasons to buy," one retail executive said, noting that summer is increasingly becoming a "promotion-only season" for makeup.

Analysts expect the push from retailers to result in multiple nationwide beauty campaigns between mid-June and mid-July, particularly from established brands aiming to protect shelf presence.
